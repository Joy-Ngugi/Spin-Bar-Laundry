How to Properly Wash and Care for Different Fabrics

Your clothes last longer when you treat each fabric the right way. One of the biggest reasons clothes fade, shrink, or lose shape is because many people wash everything the same way. The truth is: different fabrics need different care. The good news? You don’t need expensive products—just the right habits.

1) Cotton (T-shirts, shirts, bedsheets)

Cotton is strong and common in Kenyan homes, but it can shrink or fade if washed in very hot water. For everyday cotton items, cold or lukewarm water is usually enough.

  • Wash in cold or warm water to prevent shrinking.
  • Separate whites and coloured cotton to avoid colour transfer.
  • Dry in shade for bright colours to reduce fading.

2) Denim (Jeans)

Jeans don’t need frequent washing. Overwashing denim makes it fade faster and lose its shape. Spot-clean small stains and wash only when necessary.

  • Turn jeans inside out before washing.
  • Use cold water and gentle cycle.
  • Air-dry instead of using high heat.

3) Wool (Sweaters, knitwear)

Wool is delicate and can easily shrink if washed in hot water or rough cycles. If you’re not sure, it’s safer to hand wash or use a gentle wash setting.

  • Use cold water and mild detergent.
  • Do not twist or wring wool items.
  • Dry flat to maintain shape.

4) Polyester & synthetics (Sportswear, uniforms)

Synthetic fabrics dry fast and are easy to care for, but they hold sweat smell if not washed properly. Avoid heavy fabric softeners because they can trap odours in sportswear.

  • Wash soon after use to avoid odour build-up.
  • Use warm or cold water.
  • Avoid too much softener on sportswear.

5) Delicates (Silk, lace, innerwear)

Delicate fabrics need gentle care. Rough washing can stretch them, tear them, or cause them to lose their smooth texture.

  • Use a laundry bag if using a washing machine.
  • Hand wash in cold water when possible.
  • Dry away from direct sunlight.

Quick Tip:

Always check clothing labels before washing. A small symbol can save your favourite outfit from shrinking, fading, or tearing.
