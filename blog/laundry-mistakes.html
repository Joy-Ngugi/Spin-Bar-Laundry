Common Laundry Mistakes That Ruin Clothes

Have you ever bought a nice outfit, washed it a few times, then it suddenly looks old and tired? Most clothes don’t get ruined because they are “bad quality”—they get ruined because of small laundry mistakes that happen every day. Let’s fix them.

1) Using too much detergent

More detergent doesn’t mean cleaner clothes. Too much detergent leaves residue, makes clothes feel stiff, and can trap dirt instead of removing it.

2) Mixing whites and colours

This is one of the fastest ways to ruin your whites. Dark clothes can bleed colour, especially during the first few washes.

3) Overloading the washing machine

When the drum is too full, clothes don’t have space to move. That means less cleaning, more wrinkles, and more wear on the fabric.

4) Washing with hot water always

Hot water can shrink fabrics and fade colours faster. Cold or warm water works well for most everyday laundry, especially if the detergent is good.

5) Not checking pockets

Coins, tissues, pens, and receipts can cause big damage. Tissue breaks into tiny pieces and sticks to everything, while a pen can stain an entire load.

6) Drying everything in direct sunlight

Sunlight is great for drying, but it can fade dark clothes and printed fabrics. A smart trick is to turn clothes inside out or dry them in shade.

Laundry checklist before you press “Start”:

  • Sort whites, colours, and dark clothes
  • Check pockets (especially tissues and pens)
  • Don’t overload the machine
  • Use the correct detergent amount
  • Pick the right cycle (gentle for delicate items)
